Hey, I’m Sam Dillin. I’m a Software Developer from Harding University. I have a bachelor’s in Computer Science and finished with a 2.7 GPA. My education covers an assortment of back-end and front-end development. Most of what I learned was theoretical practice with real world examples.
During college I was dedicated to make my education successful. It was enjoyable to see the fruits of my efforts as I progressed. Every new class added another tool in my toolbelt. My focus was to learn skills that I would be able to use myself.
For my junior year in college, I picked up a part time job working as a cook at my local pizza place. It was difficult maintaining a 15hr part time job while being a full time student with 15-16hrs. To me, I think these classes were designed to weed people out. However I was able to succeed by maintaining a schedule and sticking to it. The key for me was working ahead of time instead of close to the deadline.
I still have much to learn as I gain more experience. With my current knowledge, I feel like I have a good base to start from. My work ethic and experience with others have taught me valuable lessons as well. For any project, I’ll be able to get the job done.
